Takeaways

Now that you have learned all about wireframes, here are some tips for you to create your own!

  1. Keep color to a minimum. The use of multiple colors can be distracting, so it is generally advised to stick to one color or multiple shades of one color. Though it may be tempting to have a spin at the color wheel, it’s best to go monochrome (at least for now).


  2. Find the tool that’s right for you. A multitude of wireframing tools are out there to make your work easier! Downloadable software like Adobe XD and Sketch can give you the flexibility to modify files on your PC with ease. On the other hand, online tools like Figma and Mockplus will save you the hassle of installing software. Or maybe you’d like to go old-school with paper and pen! There is no right or wrong medium - it all depends on what you’re comfortable with and what kind of wireframe you’re looking to create.


  3. Be consistent. The best websites have a central theme that ties the whole site together. Develop a theme and make it reflect in your wireframe. Try not to have too many size variations - for example, section headings look best when they are all the same size.


  4. Keep it simple. Your clients will best understand the purpose of your design when they can see past all the extraneous design elements. Extravagant graphics and flowy, script-like fonts can look beautiful - but you’ll want to save these for the prototype!


  5. Develop a narrative for the user to follow. You are the writer, and your user is the main character in your story. When designing a wireframe, ask yourself what you want the user to do. Maybe they will enter the website and scroll down to learn more. Maybe they will click a button to donate. Or maybe they will be inspired to share something of their own. Put yourself in your user’s shoes and imagine yourself navigating your website.


  6. Remember the Gestalt principles. Principles like Alignment, Similarity, Grouping, and Proximity can significantly aid a visual layout. But don’t limit yourself! The more principles you apply, the stronger your design will be.


  7. Keep looking for inspiration along the way! Behance and Dribbble are your friends. You can find all kinds of wireframes created by professional designers and students. Pinterest is another great place to look for inspiration.
